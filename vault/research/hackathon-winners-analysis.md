# Hackathon Winners Analysis

## What Judges Actually Reward

Based on analysis of 20+ sustainability hackathon winners across Devpost, MLH, and Google-sponsored events:

### Pattern 1: Specificity Beats Generality
Winners solve ONE narrow problem deeply, not "sustainability" broadly.
- Waste Watcher AI (Cornell 1st place) — camera above plate return to measure food waste with AI
- AviWind Guardian (Cloudera 1st place) — protecting migratory birds from wind turbine collisions
- Shamsi (AI for Sustainability 1st place) — optimizing solar energy with climate-responsive AI

### Pattern 2: Real-Time + Visual = "Wow Factor"
Judges want to SEE the AI doing something impressive in the demo.
- EarthLens (GenAI Genesis 2025 winner) — real-time satellite data for disaster prediction
- FireSight (GenAI Genesis 2025 winner) — satellite to wildfire risk with natural language summaries
- Ecobot (MLH winner) — YOLOv4 detecting ocean plastic with bounding boxes

### Pattern 3: Novel AI Application, Not a Wrapper
Projects that just call GPT for text generation lose. Winners use AI as the core mechanism.
- Computer vision detecting waste/wildlife
- ML models predicting energy patterns
- AI analyzing sensor/satellite data in real time

### Pattern 4: Actionable Output
Winners produce decisions, not just information.
- CarbonAltDel — tells restaurants WHICH ingredients to swap + carbon savings
- Crops+ — recommends WHICH crops to plant based on soil analysis
- GreenLens — scans products and suggests SPECIFIC sustainable alternatives

## GenAI Genesis 2025 Winners (Last Year)

- **Overall:** ThoughtMirror (AI journaling)
- **Sustainability:** EarthLens (disaster prediction), GreenLens (product scanning), FireSight (wildfire risk)

Key insight: Last year's sustainability winners were satellite/disaster focused. Product-scanning (GreenLens) also placed. To differentiate in 2026, avoid repeating these exact concepts.

## What's Been Done to Death (AVOID)

- Generic carbon footprint calculators (dozens exist: Carbon27, CO2AI, GreenTrack)
- Simple receipt scanning for carbon (seCOia, Reewild already do this)
- Basic chatbot giving eco-tips
- Simple waste sorting classifiers
- Generic "plant a tree" gamification

## What Hasn't Been Done Well Yet

- AI analyzing SUPPLY CHAIN sustainability of products (not just carbon, but water, labor, land use)
- AI-powered policy/regulation compliance for businesses
- Real-time community-level environmental impact (neighborhood/city scale)
- AI agent that autonomously researches and recommends sustainable alternatives with reasoning
- AI that turns complex climate data into actionable personal/business decisions
